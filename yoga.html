Surya Namaskar: The 12-Step Sun Salutation

Surya Namaskar, or Sun Salutation, is a sequence of 12 powerful yoga poses. It is a full-body workout that warms up the body, stretches the muscles, and links breath with movement.

The 12-Step Sequence

Follow these steps in a fluid, continuous motion, breathing deeply as you move from one pose to the next. The sequence is symmetrical, with steps 8-12 mirroring steps 2-6.

1. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Stand at the top of your mat, feet together. Bring your palms together in a prayer position at your chest. Inhale and exhale deeply.

2. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Inhale, raise your arms overhead, and gently arch your back. Keep your hips forward and gaze towards your hands.

3. Hastapadasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

Exhale and hinge from your hips to fold forward, bringing your hands to the floor. Bend your knees if needed to keep your back flat.

4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Inhale, step your right foot back into a deep lunge. Lower your hips and look up, keeping your chest open. Your hands are on the floor for support.

5. Chaturanga Dandasana (Four-Limbed Staff Pose)

Exhale, step your left foot back to meet the right. Shift your weight forward and lower your body into a push-up position, keeping your elbows tucked in.

6. Ashtanga Namaskar (Eight-Limbed Salutation)

Bring your knees, chest, and chin to the floor, keeping your hips lifted. This pose touches eight parts of your body to the mat.

7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Inhale, slide forward and up into a gentle backbend. Press your feet and hips into the floor, lift your chest, and keep your shoulders relaxed.

8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

Exhale, tuck your toes, and lift your hips up and back into an inverted V-shape. Stretch your spine and feel the stretch in your hamstrings and calves.

9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Equestrian Pose)

Inhale, step your left foot forward between your hands into a lunge. Repeat the motion from step 4, but with the opposite leg forward.

10. Hastapadasana (Hand to Foot Pose)

Exhale and bring your right foot forward to meet your left, folding forward again into Hastapadasana.

11. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)

Inhale, sweep your arms up and overhead, gently arching your back and lifting your chest. Gaze upwards.

12. Pranamasana (Prayer Pose)

Exhale, bring your hands back to a prayer position at your chest, returning to the starting pose. This completes one full round.

Overall Benefits of Surya Namaskar

Practicing Surya Namaskar is a holistic approach to fitness that provides a wide range of benefits for both your mind and body.

  • Full-Body Warm-up: The sequence acts as a dynamic warm-up, preparing your muscles and joints for more intense activity.
  • Increased Flexibility: The fluid movements stretch the entire body, from your hamstrings and back to your shoulders and hips.
  • Strengthens Muscles: It builds strength in your core, arms, shoulders, and legs.
  • Improves Cardiovascular Health: The continuous flow and synchronized breathing elevate your heart rate and improve blood circulation.
  • Calms the Mind: Linking breath to movement helps calm the nervous system and improves mental focus and clarity.
